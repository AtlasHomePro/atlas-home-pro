About

Built by owners, for owners.

The contractor we wish we'd been able to call, now running out of Los Angeles.

A Letter from the Founder

After 30 years of owning and operating multifamily real estate in Los Angeles, we built Atlas because we were tired of calling the contractor we had and decided to become the contractor we always wanted. We operate across seven trades through a combination of in-house teams and trusted partners, serving our own portfolio, the networks we've built over three decades, and the owners and operators who find their way to us.

I grew up on job sites. My father taught me that a building is a living thing — it asks for attention whether you give it or not. For most of my career I was on the receiving end of contractor relationships: the late calls, the changed invoices, the quiet substitutions no one told us about until the finish didn't match. I kept thinking someone should fix this. Eventually I realized the person who should fix it was me.

Atlas is small by design. Every project passes through a general contractor who has, personally, owned and operated the kind of building you're asking us to work on. We answer our own phones. We walk buildings before we quote them. We quote honestly and we hold the number. When something goes wrong — and on old LA housing stock, something occasionally does — you hear about it from us first.

If that sounds like how you'd want your contractor to operate, we should probably talk.

— David Safai

Principles
How we operate, distilled.
01

Owner-led oversight

Every project passes through a general contractor who has personally owned and operated buildings like yours. No handoffs to unknown site leads.

02

Honest quotes

We walk the building before we price it. The number we quote is the number we hold — change orders exist only when scope does.

03

Trades under one roof

HVAC, plumbing, electrical, painting, water restoration, gates and garage doors. Seven trades, one point of accountability.

04

Occupied-building discipline

Most of what we touch is tenanted. Our crews are trained to work around residents, not around them.

05

Pick up the phone

We answer our own phones. If a water event hits at 2am, the person on the other end of the line is the person running the response.

06

Bad news first

When something goes wrong — and on old LA housing stock, something occasionally does — you hear about it from us before you hear about it from anyone else.
