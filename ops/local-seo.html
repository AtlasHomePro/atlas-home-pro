The Core Idea

Every completed job produces a geo-tagged, service-specific content asset that publishes to the website AND Google Business Profile. Real jobs → real content → real rankings. Generic SEO content is dead. The algorithm now rewards operational reality over manipulated text.

The 3 Pillars
Pillar 1

Algorithmic Real Estate

Your physical office location dictates your digital ceiling. Never share a radius with an entrenched competitor. Never use a residential address. Target affluent demographics.
Pillar 2

Reputation Engine

Google evaluates trust across the entire web. A great Google profile with a bad Yelp profile = algorithmic trust issue. Minimum 10-20 reviews on BOTH platforms just to register.
Pillar 3

Proof-Based Content Flywheel

Every job triggers: tech captures photos → system applies geo-metadata and tags service type → content auto-publishes to geo-page on site AND as a GBP post.

Possum Filter Rules — Do Not Violate

  • Never share a radius with an entrenched competitor in the same service category
  • Never use a residential or apartment address for GBP (guarantees suspension)
  • Never open a second location within 200 yards of a dominant competitor
  • Never rely on GBP alone — PPC + LSA + Organic must run together
What Techs Do on Every Job (Manual — Cannot Be Automated)

Field Crew Duties

This is the raw material that powers the entire flywheel. No photos = no content = no rankings.
1
Take before/after photos + short client video at every job
This is non-negotiable. Tie it to technician incentives.
2
Log the job in Airtable: neighborhood, service type, short description
If it's not logged, it doesn't exist in the content pipeline.
3
Ask the customer for a review in person before leaving
In-person ask converts 10x higher than automated follow-up. Automation handles the reminder after.
David's Manual Tasks (One-Time or Periodic)

Owner Tasks

Things only you can do — accounts, budgets, vendor decisions.
D
Claim top 10-20 directory profiles: Yelp, Thumbtack, Angi, BBB, HomeAdvisor
Only high-traffic directories. Not hundreds of low-quality citations.
D
Sign up for a geogrid tool (Local Falcon or Local Viking)
~$40-80/mo. Visualizes where you rank #1 vs. where you're invisible.
D
Set up and fund Google LSA (requires license verification)
LSA = top of page above PPC. Google Guaranteed badge. Pays per lead not per click.
D
Set up and fund Google PPC campaigns
Target neighborhoods where organic/GBP rank is weak (red zones on geogrid).
D
Create technician review incentive program (bonus per review, leaderboard)
Review generation must be a top-down, incentivized first principle of training.
