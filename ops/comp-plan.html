Compensation Philosophy
Core principle: Attract winners, weed out coasters, and tie every dollar of incentive pay to metrics that directly grow enterprise value. This is Tommy Mello's system adapted for Atlas.

02
The 4-KPI System — Must Hit All 4

If you only track revenue, techs oversell and rush. All four KPIs must work together or you pay more than you should — and your PE multiple suffers from bad reviews, callbacks, and margin erosion.

KPI 01
Revenue / Avg Ticket
Job revenue generated per van per month. Primary earning driver. Good-Better-Best pricing lifts this directly.
Plumbing: $28K+ · HVAC: $40K+
Tool: HouseCall Pro — van revenue report
KPI 02
Close Rate
Jobs closed ÷ jobs quoted in-home. Low closers get coached or cycled out.
Target: ≥ 60% (A1 runs 62%)
Tool: HouseCall Pro — estimates vs. won
KPI 03
Callback Rate
% of jobs requiring return visit within 30 days. Protects quality. Penalizes rushing.
Target: ≤ 3% (Gold: 0–2%)
Tool: HouseCall Pro — callback tag
KPI 04
Customer Reviews
Google 5-star reviews per month. Million-dollar techs all maintain 4.5+ average.
≥ 4.6 avg · 3+ new reviews/mo/tech
Tool: Chiirp auto-request + Google Business
Rule: ALL 4 KPIs must be in target range to unlock the full bonus tier. A tech with great revenue but a 6% callback rate drops to Base Tier regardless of sales. Non-negotiable. Techs see their real-time scorecard in HouseCall Pro — transparency is the management tool.

How to Sell This Comp Plan to Techs
Tommy Mello's warning: The hardest part isn't building the comp plan — it's convincing your best techs to embrace it. Frame it correctly from Day 1 in the interview, not after they've been hired on flat hourly.
The Recruiting Pitch — Word for Word
"Most companies pay you the same $30 an hour whether you're amazing or average. We don't do that. Here, your base is competitive — but what separates our top guys is that they're making $90,000 to $110,000 a year because they know how to do the job right, present options professionally, and leave every customer wanting to call us back.

We track four numbers — your revenue, your close rate, your callback rate, and your reviews. Hit all four and you're in Gold tier. Miss one and we work on it together. The techs who love this place are the ones who are confident in their work and want to get paid like it."
The 3 Questions That Identify A-Players
"Our top techs make $25K–$40K in bonuses on top of base. Does that excite or worry you?"
✅ A-Player Answer
"That excites me — I'm good at this and I want to be paid for it."
❌ Wrong Answer
"How guaranteed is the base?" — These people optimize for safety, not performance. Move on.
"When a customer says no to your recommendation, what do you do?"
✅ A-Player Answer
Explains options again, asks what it would take, offers to get a manager on the phone. Doesn't give up on first no. Shows curiosity about the objection.
❌ Wrong Answer
"I just move on." — No fight, no curiosity. Wrong for Atlas. These techs leave money on the table every single job.
"What's your average Google review rating at your last job?"
✅ A-Player Answer
Knows the number. Can give specific examples of how they earned 5-star reviews. Reviews are part of their identity.
❌ Wrong Answer
Doesn't know or says reviews aren't their job. This tech will drag your rating and cost you leads.
Red flag: If a candidate spends the whole interview asking about guaranteed hours, overtime rules, and PTO — and shows zero curiosity about the bonus structure — they will be your lowest performer. Thank them and move on.
