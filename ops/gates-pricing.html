📖 Service Guides, Tech Reference
What to check, what to say, what to upsell on every gate and garage job.
🔧 Garage Door Spring Guide
Torsion springs (above door): Most common in LA. One or two springs. ALWAYS replace both when one breaks, the other is at end of life.
Extension springs (sides): Older homes. Replace with torsion if possible, safer and lasts longer. Upgrade pitch.
Cycle ratings: Standard = 10,000 cycles. Heavy duty = 25,000. High-cycle = 50,000 (estate/commercial). Always quote heavy duty or high-cycle on Best tier.
Safety cable: Extension springs MUST have safety cables. If missing, add to every quote.
🚪 Garage Door Opener Guide
Drive types: Chain (noisy, cheap), Belt (quiet, mid), Direct drive (quietest, premium). Beverly Hills = always belt or direct drive.
Horsepower: 1/2 HP for standard, 3/4 HP for heavy or insulated doors, 1 HP+ for commercial.
LiftMaster is preferred brand: myQ app integration, battery backup option, safety sensors standard.
Battery backup: $50 part, $30 labor, always include in Better/Best. LA power outages = stranded cars without it.
🚪 Electric Gate Diagnosis Guide
Motor not running: Check power first, then capacitor, then motor windings, then control board. Board replacement is expensive, get part cost before quoting repair.
Gate moves slow/jerky: Check battery backup if solar, lubricate drive mechanism, check limit switches.
Gate won't close fully: Limit switch or obstruction sensor. Often a 10-minute fix, quote accordingly.
Safety sensors: Mandatory on all electric gates. If missing or damaged = always replace as part of any repair.
🏰 Beverly Hills / Estate Standards
Always Best tier minimum for BH, Bel Air, Brentwood estate work.
Custom gates: Always site assess. Iron/steel vs. aluminum vs. wood. HOA approval may be needed.
Access control: Intercom with camera + smartphone app is the standard. Ring or DoorBird preferred.
Permit: Required for new gate installation in LA. Add $200-$300 to quote.
Lubrication type: Use white lithium grease on springs and hinges. Silicone spray on rubber seals. Never WD-40, it attracts dirt.
