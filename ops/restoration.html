Business Model

How Water Restoration Works

Three services, two license tracks, one payment system. Insurance pays for most of it — which is why certifications, Xactimate, and TPA network access are the keys to revenue.

Mitigation

Emergency water extraction, demo of damaged materials, initial drying setup. First on scene, first to bill.

  • Requires IICRC certs (WRT minimum)
  • 60-minute response standard

Drying

Structural drying with monitoring over 3-5 days. Documented daily with moisture readings and Xactimate line items.

  • Requires ASD certification
  • Daily documentation critical for billing

Reconstruction

Rebuild damaged structures — drywall, flooring, paint. Covered by Atlas's Class B contractor license.

  • CSLB Class B license (already active)
  • Extends job value significantly
Insurance companies pay through TPAs (Third-Party Administrators). Without IICRC certs, Xactimate, and TPA network approvals, Atlas cannot receive dispatched claims.

Water Categories and Requirements

Category 1
Clean Water
Supply lines, faucets, rain. Standard rates. WRT certification required.
Category 2
Gray Water
Dishwashers, washing machines, toilet overflow. Higher rates. Requires AMRT certification.
Category 3
Black Water
Sewage, flooding, standing water. Highest rates. Requires AMRT + CPL insurance.

Critical Path

Seven things that unlock the entire business. Nothing else matters until these are done.

1

Pass WRT Certification

Water Restoration Technician. First cert every TPA and carrier requires. 3 days, livestream available.

2

Register Atlas as IICRC Certified Firm

Required for TPA applications. Needs 1 WRT-certified tech + GL insurance on file. $25 app + $199/year.

3

Complete ASD Certification

Applied Structural Drying. In-person only, 3 days. Required for proper drying documentation and billing.

4

Add CPL Insurance

Contractors Pollution Liability. Required for mold and Category 3 water. Without it, no black water jobs.

5

Activate Xactimate Pro

Industry-standard estimating software. Carriers require it. Build templates for Cat 1, 2, and 3 losses.

6

Complete AMRT by Day 90

Applied Microbial Remediation. In-person, 4 days. Unlocks mold jobs, Cat 2/3 water, and higher payouts.

7

Submit TPA Applications

Contractor Connection, Alacrity, Westhill, Accuserve. This is how insurance claims get routed to Atlas.

Training

Certification Path

Three certifications in order. Each one unlocks new job types and higher revenue. Do not skip any.

Online / Livestream

WRT

Water Restoration Technician

  • 24 hours / 3 days
  • Livestream option available
  • Complete in first 2 weeks of hire
  • Pass exam within 45 days
Unlocks: IICRC Firm registration, TPA applications, Cat 1 water jobs
In-Person Only

ASD

Applied Structural Drying

  • 21 hours / 3 days
  • In-person only — cannot skip
  • Complete within 60 days of hire
  • Pass exam immediately after
Unlocks: Proper drying documentation, structural drying billing
In-Person Only

AMRT

Applied Microbial Remediation

  • 28 hours / 4 days
  • In-person only
  • Complete by day 90
  • Required for mold + Cat 2/3 water
Unlocks: Mold jobs, gray/black water, higher payouts
IICRC Firm Registration: As soon as the first tech passes WRT, register Atlas as an IICRC Certified Firm. Requirements: at least 1 active IICRC-certified tech, GL insurance on file. Cost: $25 application + $199/year renewal.
Supplemental certs (later): HST, FSRT, OCT, CCT. Long-term target: Master Water Restorer path after WRT + ASD + AMRT + HST + CCT/CCMT.
