Home Security Gates for Westside LA — Neighborhood-by-Neighborhood Guide

Gate style, materials, and access control aren't one-size-fits-all. What works in Bel Air doesn't work in West Hollywood. This guide covers what each Westside neighborhood needs.

Atlas Home Pro 8 min read Published April 8, 2026 Licensed & Insured
Licensed & Insured Permit Coordination Included Westside LA Specialists Dispatch from Fairfax (90036)

A driveway gate does two things: it controls access, and it communicates something about the property. In a neighborhood like Bel Air (90077), an ornate wrought iron gate on stone pillars is expected and appropriate. In West Hollywood (90046), the same gate on a 1940s bungalow would look out of place. Getting the gate right means understanding the architectural character of your neighborhood, your HOA's requirements if applicable, and the operational demands of your specific property.

Beverly Hills (90210) — Estate Standards

Beverly Hills sets the standard for formal driveway gate design in Los Angeles. Properties in 90210 typically call for ornate wrought iron or custom fabricated steel, often 10–14 feet wide configured as dual-swing or bi-folding gates mounted on substantial concrete pillars. Gate height commonly runs 6–8 feet. The visual weight and finish quality that reads as appropriate in Beverly Hills — hand-forged details, premium powder coat, stone-clad or cast concrete pillars — would be architecturally excessive in almost any other Westside zip code.

City of Beverly Hills requires permits for new gate installations and significant modifications, and many properties are also subject to HOA or architectural review committee approval. Plan for that process to add 2–6 weeks before fabrication can begin. Access control at this level typically means license plate recognition (LPR) combined with video intercom and remote management capability — a system that logs entry, allows remote guest access, and integrates with a property management platform.

Budget range for a complete Beverly Hills gate system — fabrication, pillars, underground hydraulic motor, and full access control — runs $15,000–$60,000. The high end of that range reflects pillar construction on properties with existing stone or masonry features, fully custom fabrication, and multi-camera video intercom with cloud management. The lower end covers a quality custom gate with electromechanical arm operator and a video intercom with remote access.

Bel Air (90077) — Hillside Estate Challenges

Bel Air shares Beverly Hills' preference for substantial custom iron gates, but the hillside terrain introduces installation complexity that flat-lot properties don't face. Grade changes along a driveway apron complicate the choice between swing and sliding gates. A swing gate requires level ground across its arc of travel — even a modest slope can cause the gate to drag, bind, or gap at the ground. Sliding gates don't have this problem: the gate travels parallel to a fixed ground track, and grade doesn't affect its operation.

On hillside properties in 90077, sliding gates are often the practical solution even when the homeowner's aesthetic preference leans toward swing. When swing gates are specified, the driveway apron typically requires grading or a custom gate leaf design that accounts for the terrain. Either way, the motor post footing on a hillside installation often requires engineered specifications — the soil conditions that come with hillside construction don't allow a standard post footing without engineering review.

The mature tree canopy in Bel Air also affects installation planning. Conduit runs for electrical service to gate operators frequently need to cover longer distances than on flat-lot properties, navigating around root systems and following a path that doesn't damage established landscaping. Budget for Bel Air runs $12,000–$45,000 depending on gate size, terrain complexity, and the access control system specified.

Brentwood (90049) — Ranch and Traditional

The characteristic Brentwood gate is more restrained than its Beverly Hills neighbor: black powder-coated tubular iron on painted wood or concrete columns, framed by the lush landscaping that defines the neighborhood. Traditional swing or sliding designs predominate. The architectural context — ranch houses, Monterey colonials, traditional two-stories with mature trees — calls for gates that integrate with the property rather than announce themselves.

HOA restrictions in Brentwood are generally less prescriptive than in Beverly Hills, which gives homeowners more latitude on design without a formal approval process. The market here is also more cost-conscious than BH or Bel Air; homeowners expect quality but aren't specifying the same level of custom fabrication. Standard tubular iron in classic designs, powder-coated in black or dark bronze, with a reliable electromechanical arm operator and video intercom with keypad access represents the typical Brentwood installation.

Budget range for a complete Brentwood system: $8,000–$25,000. The lower end covers a standard tubular iron swing gate on wood columns with a basic keypad and intercom. The upper end includes custom fabrication, concrete columns, and a more capable access control system.

West Hollywood (90046, 90048) — Compact Lots, Modern Style

West Hollywood's density creates gate installation conditions that differ significantly from the larger estate lots to the north and west. Lots in 90046 and 90048 are frequently narrower, with less driveway setback from the street. Where swing gates require clearance equal to the width of the gate leaf, sliding gates need only a parallel run alongside the driveway. On compact WeHo lots, sliding gates are often the only practical option.

The architectural vocabulary of West Hollywood has also shifted substantially over the past decade — renovated 1940s bungalows, new-build contemporary infill, and mid-century modern properties make up a significant share of the housing stock. Contemporary flat-bar iron or aluminum gate designs match this aesthetic without the period incongruity that would come from an ornate wrought iron gate. Heights are lower, designs are cleaner, and the visual integration with the property matters more than formal estate presence.

West Hollywood has setback requirements for gates that affect where along the driveway a gate can be installed. Confirm clearances with the city before finalizing placement. Budget range: $5,000–$18,000, reflecting the smaller gate sizes, simpler motor configurations, and more compact access control setups appropriate for this market.

The architectural context of your neighborhood determines what looks right. A gate that would be understated in Beverly Hills would be out of scale in West Hollywood — and vice versa.

Westwood (90025) — University Adjacency and Mixed Use

Westwood spans residential blocks near UCLA and a mix of mid-rise condos, apartment buildings, and single-family homes closer to the 405. Residential gate work in 90025 tends toward the practical — powder-coated tubular iron or aluminum sliding gates on smaller lots, with access control focused on reliability over sophistication. Properties near the university corridor often have pedestrian traffic considerations that estate neighborhoods don't: a gate system may need to manage both vehicle and foot access at the same entry point.

Single-family properties in Westwood's quieter residential blocks follow patterns similar to Brentwood — traditional designs, moderate budgets, and reliable systems. Condominium and multi-unit properties are more likely to be served through building management, with common gate systems shared across multiple units. Budget for residential Westwood installations: $6,000–$20,000 depending on configuration.

Pacific Palisades (90272) — Coastal and Fire Zone Considerations

Post-fire rebuilds in Pacific Palisades are incorporating gate specifications that weren't standard practice before. Emergency breakaway features — mechanisms that allow firefighters to force entry without a gate code — are being specified on new installations where access was previously a concern during emergency response. Some rebuilt properties are also choosing aluminum over steel or iron for the gate structure itself, primarily to address corrosion from coastal salt air. Aluminum doesn't rust, requires less maintenance over time, and holds powder coat finishes well in marine environments.

The fire access consideration extends beyond hardware: gate placement and the width of the opening matter for emergency vehicle access. Consulting with the local fire station about clearance standards before finalizing a gate design is a step that more Palisades homeowners are taking after recent fire events. Budget range in 90272: $8,000–$28,000, with rebuild projects at the higher end given their scope and the additional specifications involved.

Marina del Rey (90292) — HOA and Condo Context

Marina del Rey's housing stock is predominantly high-density condominiums and townhome complexes, most of which are governed by HOAs that own and maintain shared gate systems. Individual homeowners in 90292 rarely install private driveway gates — the lot configurations and HOA governance structures make private gate installation uncommon. The relevant work in this market is HOA common gate repair, access control upgrades, and video intercom system replacements for existing shared entry systems.

A typical Marina del Rey engagement involves upgrading the video intercom on an existing HOA gate — replacing an aging wired system with a modern IP-based intercom that residents can answer from their phones, with cloud-based access logs for property management. These upgrades run $2,500–$8,000 depending on the number of units served, the existing wiring infrastructure, and the intercom system specified. Atlas works directly with HOA boards and property management companies on this type of project.

Permit Requirements by City

Permit requirements vary by jurisdiction across the Westside, and they matter — an unpermitted gate can create issues at the time of sale and expose you to stop-work orders if a neighbor complains. Here's the breakdown for the zip codes covered in this guide:

Beverly Hills (90210): Permits required for new gate installations and significant modifications. The City of Beverly Hills Building & Safety Department handles gate permits. Plan for additional review time if the property is in a HOA or architectural review area.

Bel Air (90077): The area straddles unincorporated Los Angeles County and City of Los Angeles jurisdiction. For unincorporated LA County parcels, permits are required for new structures including gates. For City of LA parcels, a permit is required for gates over 6 feet in height. Verify jurisdiction for your specific parcel before applying.

Brentwood (90049) and Westwood (90025): These are City of Los Angeles jurisdictions. Permits are required for gates over 6 feet. Standard residential permits are typically straightforward, though properties in hillside overlay zones may have additional review requirements.

West Hollywood (90046, 90048): The City of West Hollywood has setback requirements for gates in addition to permit requirements. Confirm placement before finalizing gate design — the setback from the property line affects where the gate can be installed.

Pacific Palisades (90272): City of Los Angeles jurisdiction. Fire zone overlay designations affect some properties; consult with a contractor familiar with Palisades post-fire rebuild requirements. Permits required for new gate installations.

Marina del Rey (90292): County of Los Angeles jurisdiction for most parcels. Permits required for new gate structures. HOA approval is typically required before any permit application for properties in governed communities.

HOA approval timelines typically run 2–6 weeks for standard gate installations. Build this into your project timeline before scheduling fabrication. Atlas handles permit coordination as part of every installation — we pull the permit, schedule inspections, and deliver a permitted installation.
