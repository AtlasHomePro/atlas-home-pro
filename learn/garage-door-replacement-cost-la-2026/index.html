Garage Door Replacement Cost in Los Angeles — 2026 Guide

What you'll actually pay to replace a garage door in LA. Published prices across styles, materials, and zip codes — no bait-and-switch.

Atlas Home Pro April 8, 2026 8 min read Licensed & Insured
Licensed & Insured Same-Day Available Published Pricing West LA Specialists

Cost transparency is rare in the garage door industry. Contractors routinely advertise low door prices while burying labor, disposal, and opener charges in the fine print — meaning a $599 door suddenly becomes a $2,400 invoice at the end of the job. This guide publishes actual installed prices for Los Angeles in 2026 — not national averages that don't account for LA labor rates, permit requirements in cities like Beverly Hills and Santa Monica, or the access complexity common to hillside properties and estate homes throughout the Westside. Use this to budget accurately and identify when a quote is off before you sign anything.

What Garage Door Replacement Costs in LA

Los Angeles installed costs run 20–35% above national averages. The gap is driven by three factors: union-scale and premium labor market rates, permit requirements on certain property types in Beverly Hills (90210), Bel Air (90077), and neighboring cities, and the access complexity that comes with hillside driveways, steep grades, and custom-framed openings in estate properties. A contractor pricing a job in Kansas City is quoting a different cost basis than one working in Brentwood (90049) or West Hollywood (90046).

For planning purposes: a standard single-car door runs $1,200–$2,200 installed in LA. A double-car door — the most common replacement job — lands in the $1,800–$3,500 range. Estate and custom doors, which represent a significant share of work in Beverly Hills and Bel Air, start around $4,000 and reach $12,000 or more for fully custom fabrications with motorized systems and permit processing.

What's Included in "Installed" Price

When Atlas quotes an installed price, it covers: removal and haul-away of the old door, the new door and all associated hardware (hinges, rollers, tracks, cables), a new opener if you're replacing the existing unit, standard wiring for the opener, and cleanup. The price you see is the price you pay for a standard residential opening at grade.

Certain conditions trigger additional line items that are quoted separately before any work begins. A new opener when replacing only the door — not already included in a door-only quote — runs $350–$650 depending on motor size and smart-home features. Permits in Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, and Culver City add $150–$400 to the job; we handle the filing. Spring replacement, when the existing springs are approaching end of cycle life, adds $195–$395 — we'll tell you their estimated remaining cycles during the assessment. A wireless keypad or smart opener upgrade runs $150–$350. Weatherstripping upgrade, which matters for energy efficiency in garages converted to living space, adds $95–$195.

Beverly Hills, Bel Air & Westside LA — What's Different

Estate properties in Beverly Hills (90210), Bel Air (90077), Brentwood (90049), and West Hollywood (90046) frequently involve installation conditions that don't apply to standard residential jobs. Opening dimensions are one of the most common factors: many homes in these zip codes have 9-foot or 10-foot wide openings, or 8-foot tall openings — versus the standard 8x7 configuration. A non-standard opening requires a custom-ordered or custom-fabricated door, which adds lead time and cost.

HOA architectural approval is another variable throughout Bel Air, Brentwood, and gated communities across the Westside. A door replacement that changes materials, finish, or panel style typically requires written HOA approval, which adds 2–4 weeks to the project timeline. We prepare the documentation package on your behalf, but the timeline is outside our control. Hillside driveways — common throughout Bel Air and Laurel Canyon — can complicate installation when significant grade exists between the driveway and the opening threshold. Beverly Hills requires permits for door replacements on certain property types, which we confirm during the initial assessment. Taken together, these factors typically add $500–$2,000 to jobs in premium zip codes, and that range is reflected in the estate tier of the pricing table above.

Garage Door Materials — What to Buy in LA

Steel is the most common and cost-effective material for garage door replacement throughout LA. It holds up well in LA's climate, accepts paint finishes that hold color under UV exposure, and carries good R-value when insulated. For most homes in Fairfax (90036), Beverly Grove (90035), Mid-City, and Westwood (90024, 90025), insulated steel is the straightforward answer.

Aluminum with marine-grade hardware is the recommended material for homes within approximately 1.5 miles of the coast. Salt air accelerates corrosion on standard steel hardware — hinges, springs, and track mounting brackets can begin corroding within 3–5 years in coastal conditions. Homes in Santa Monica, Marina del Rey, and Pacific Palisades are better served by aluminum panels with marine-grade spring and hardware packages, even at the higher price point. The service cost differential more than justifies the upfront premium over a 10-year ownership period.

Wood looks authentic on traditional architecture and is frequently specified by homeowners in Hancock Park, Larchmont Village, and Beverly Hills who are matching historical character. The maintenance consideration in LA is UV exposure — Southern California sun is hard on unfinished or under-sealed wood, and annual sealing is a real operating cost. Wood composite offers a similar aesthetic with meaningfully better resistance to warping and UV damage, and is worth considering as an alternative on south- and west-facing exposures.

Fiberglass performs well in coastal environments and resists denting, though it is more expensive than steel and represents a smaller share of the replacement market. Contemporary full-view aluminum-and-glass doors have become the signature look of remodeled Westside homes — particularly in West Hollywood (90046, 90048) and Brentwood — and command a price premium that reflects both material cost and the installation complexity of tempered glass panel management.

Opener Replacement — When to Bundle

If the existing opener is over 10 years old, replacing it at the same time as the door is worth the additional cost. The alternative — keeping the old opener and replacing it 18 months later when it fails — means paying a second service call plus a second labor premium for a job that takes about the same amount of work whether done alone or bundled with a door replacement. Bundling saves the service call fee and typically $150–$200 in labor.

LiftMaster and Chamberlain openers with myQ smart home integration run $450–$650 installed, which covers the unit, installation, app setup, and programming of remote controls and keypads. Battery backup is now standard on all openers we install — this is particularly important for properties in hillside fire evacuation zones throughout Bel Air, Brentwood, and the canyons, where power outages during red flag conditions are a real operational scenario. A garage door that won't open during an evacuation is a serious safety liability.

The 5 Questions to Ask Before Accepting a Quote

Before signing any garage door replacement quote from any contractor, get clear answers to these five questions:

  1. Is the opener included or quoted separately? Many low-ball quotes show only the door price. The opener, installation, and programming should be explicitly listed as included or excluded.
  2. What's the spring warranty? Springs should carry a minimum 10,000-cycle warranty. Premium torsion springs rated to 25,000 cycles are available and worth the upcharge on a door you expect to use twice daily for 15+ years.
  3. Is haul-away of the old door included? Disposal of a large steel or wood panel is not free. Confirm it's in the quote or you may be left with the old door on your driveway.
  4. Are permits included if required in my city? Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, and Culver City require permits for certain door replacements. The filing fee and any associated inspection time should be explicit in the quote.
  5. What's the insulation R-value? If the garage is adjacent to living space or temperature-controlled, R-value matters. Ask for the specific R-value rating of the insulation package, not just "insulated" as a descriptor.
