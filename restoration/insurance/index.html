COVERAGE GUIDE

What Homeowners Insurance Typically Covers in LA

✅ TYPICALLY COVERED
  • ✓
    Burst Pipes — Sudden pipe failure due to pressure, age, or freezing. One of the most common covered water losses in LA.
  • ✓
    Appliance Failures — Sudden leaks from washing machines, dishwashers, water heaters, and refrigerators with ice makers.
  • ✓
    Storm Water Intrusion — Wind-driven rain through a damaged roof, broken windows, or compromised flashing (with an underlying covered peril).
  • ✓
    Accidental Overflow — Toilet overflow, sink overflow, or bathtub overflow that causes damage.
  • ✓
    Mold from Covered Loss — Mold that results directly from a sudden, covered water event is generally covered as part of the same claim.
❌ TYPICALLY NOT COVERED
  • ✗
    Flood Damage — Rising ground water, storm surge, or overflowing rivers/creeks. Requires separate NFIP or private flood insurance.
  • ✗
    Gradual Leaks — Slow leaks that developed over time and were not promptly reported are typically excluded as "preventable" damage.
  • ✗
    Sewer Backup — Standard HO policies usually exclude sewer backup. A separate sewer backup endorsement (often $50-100/yr) is required.
  • ✗
    Deferred Maintenance — Damage caused by neglected repairs (cracked caulk, rusted pipes, known leaks not addressed) is generally excluded.
  • ✗
    Pre-Existing Mold — Mold that predates the policy period or is not related to a covered loss is typically excluded.
WHAT WE DOCUMENT

Atlas Home Pro's Documentation Package

A well-documented claim is a paid claim. Our documentation package is built specifically to meet the standards California insurance adjusters require.

📷

Photographic Evidence

Time-stamped photographs documenting all affected areas at arrival — before any work begins. Wide-angle room photos, close-up damage photos, and thermal imaging images of moisture behind walls. Full image archive provided to you and your adjuster.

📊

Moisture Reports

Meter readings at all affected locations documented on arrival. Daily psychrometric readings during the drying phase. Final moisture readings confirming dry standard was achieved. Format: standard drying log acceptable to all major CA carriers.

📈

Xactimate Estimates

Itemized repair estimates prepared in Xactimate — the industry-standard software used by insurance carriers and adjusters nationwide. Line-item detail for every repair component, including materials, labor, equipment, and overhead. Adjusters trust Xactimate estimates.

💻

Equipment Logs

Detailed logs of every piece of mitigation equipment deployed — dehumidifiers, air movers, air scrubbers, drying mats — including placement, quantity, dates deployed, and daily kWh readings. Required by most carriers to reimburse equipment costs.

📝

Damage Narrative

A written damage narrative describing the cause of loss, water category (clean/gray/sewage), areas affected, materials impacted, and scope of remediation performed. Written to support a claim approval, not just describe what happened.

🤝

Adjuster Coordination

We coordinate directly with your assigned adjuster — scheduling on-site inspections, responding to requests for additional information, and escalating if initial scopes are inadequate. You are kept informed at every step without needing to be the intermediary.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Insurance Claims Questions

What Los Angeles homeowners ask about water damage claims.

Do you work directly with my insurance company?

Yes. Atlas Home Pro works directly with all major homeowners insurance carriers operating in California, including State Farm, Allstate, Farmers, USAA, Chubb, Travelers, and others. We communicate directly with your adjuster, submit documentation in the format they require, and can participate in on-site inspections. You do not need to be the intermediary between your contractor and your insurer — we handle that communication on your behalf, keeping you informed at each milestone.

What is typically covered under homeowners insurance for water damage?

Standard HO-3 and HO-5 homeowners policies in California typically cover sudden and accidental water damage. This includes burst pipes, appliance supply line failures (washing machine, dishwasher, refrigerator), accidental overflow from fixtures, and water intrusion from a covered roof or wall breach. What is typically excluded: gradual leaks that went unaddressed, flood damage from rising ground water (which requires separate NFIP or private flood insurance), and damage resulting from deferred maintenance or neglect.

Do I need to pay for restoration services upfront?

For insurance claims, you typically pay your deductible only — Atlas Home Pro bills your insurer directly for the balance. We provide all documentation required by your carrier to process the claim, including itemized estimates in Xactimate format, equipment deployment logs, moisture reports, and photographic evidence. We do not require upfront payment beyond the deductible for covered claims from qualified insurers.

How do you document water damage for an insurance claim?

Our documentation package includes: arrival time-stamped photographs of all affected areas before any work begins; moisture reading logs at each affected location; thermal imaging photos identifying hidden moisture; a written damage narrative describing cause, category, and extent of the loss; an itemized repair estimate in Xactimate format; daily drying logs (psychrometric reports) showing temperature, relative humidity, and materials moisture content; and equipment deployment logs showing what equipment was placed where and for how long. This package is what adjusters expect for a well-documented claim.

What if my water damage claim is denied?

Claim denials are not always final. If your claim is denied, request a written denial letter specifying the exact policy exclusion being applied. Common grounds for appeal include: the cause of loss was mischaracterized, the extent of damage was underestimated, or the adjuster's scope of repairs is inconsistent with the actual damage. Atlas Home Pro can provide additional documentation to support a re-evaluation, and you have the right to hire a public adjuster to represent your interests in disputes. California also has the Department of Insurance complaint process available to homeowners who believe claims have been improperly handled.
