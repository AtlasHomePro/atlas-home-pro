FREQUENTLY ASKED

Water Removal Questions

Straight answers about emergency water extraction in Los Angeles.

How fast can Atlas Home Pro respond to a water emergency in Los Angeles?

Atlas Home Pro targets a 60-minute response time anywhere in the Los Angeles metro area, including Beverly Hills, West Hollywood, Silver Lake, Koreatown, and the San Fernando Valley. Our dispatch team operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including holidays. When you call, we immediately route the nearest available crew to your location. Fast response is critical because water migrates into walls, subflooring, and insulation within the first hour, dramatically increasing the scope and cost of remediation.

What equipment do you use for water extraction?

We deploy truck-mounted and portable industrial extraction units with capacities exceeding 200 gallons per minute for large-scale flooding. For tight spaces — crawl spaces, under cabinets, between walls — we use specialized wand extractors and weighted self-propelled extractor heads that recover water embedded deep in carpet padding and hardwood. All equipment meets or exceeds IICRC S500 standards for professional water damage restoration.

Is standing water in my home dangerous?

Yes, standing water presents multiple hazards. Electrically, water near outlets, appliances, or the electrical panel can cause electrocution — always shut off the main breaker before entering a flooded space if it is safe to do so. Structurally, water rapidly weakens drywall, undermines subfloor adhesives, and begins warping solid wood within hours. Biologically, mold can begin colonizing damp materials in as little as 24 to 48 hours. Category 2 (gray water from appliances) and Category 3 (sewage) water introduce harmful bacteria and pathogens into your home.

Do you handle sewage backup extraction?

Yes. Sewage backups are classified as Category 3 water — the most hazardous category — and require specialized handling. Our technicians use full personal protective equipment, industrial-grade disinfectants, and HEPA-filtered air scrubbers when dealing with sewage. All affected porous materials (drywall, insulation, carpet) must be removed and discarded rather than dried in place. We coordinate with your insurance adjuster on the proper documentation for sewage remediation claims.

Will my insurance cover emergency water removal?

Most standard homeowners insurance policies cover sudden and accidental water damage — a burst pipe, appliance failure, or storm-driven intrusion — but typically exclude gradual leaks that went unaddressed. Emergency extraction services are generally covered under the same claim as the structural damage. Atlas Home Pro provides full documentation including moisture readings, photos, and equipment logs formatted for insurance adjuster review. We also work directly with most major carriers and can assist with direct billing to expedite the claims process.
