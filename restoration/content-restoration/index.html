SALVAGE GUIDE

What Can Be Saved vs. What Must Be Replaced

Understanding salvageability helps set realistic expectations and ensures your insurance claim covers non-salvageable items at replacement value.

✅

Often Salvageable

  • ✓Solid wood furniture — Can be dried, cleaned, and refinished. Los Angeles's low humidity helps prevent warping if drying begins quickly.
  • ✓Upholstered furniture — Salvageable if exposed to clean water (Cat 1/2) and treated within 24–48 hours before microbial growth begins.
  • ✓Clothing and linens — Industrial laundering with antimicrobial treatment restores most fabrics, including delicate items and drapes.
  • ✓Hard goods & ceramics — Non-porous items clean easily; metals may require rust treatment; ceramic and glass are nearly always salvageable.
  • ✓Electronics — When powered off at the time of damage and brought to a specialist promptly, many devices can be restored through professional cleaning.
  • ✓Documents & photos — Air-dried or freeze-dried within hours, many paper documents and photographs can be recovered, including deeds and family photos.
  • ✓Area rugs — Professional rug cleaning and drying can save most wool, silk, and synthetic rugs if contamination is limited to clean water.
❌

Typically Must Be Replaced

  • ✗Particle board & MDF furniture — These materials absorb water rapidly, swell, delaminate, and cannot be dried or restored effectively.
  • ✗Mattresses — Foam and innerspring mattresses absorb contaminated water into a core that cannot be adequately cleaned or sanitized.
  • ✗Upholstered items — Cat 3 water — Sewage or floodwater contamination permanently compromises porous upholstered materials; these are a health hazard and must go.
  • ✗Pillows & stuffed items — Internal fill materials cannot be adequately sanitized; replacement is the safe and standard approach.
  • ✗Electronics — powered on while wet — Short circuits cause oxidation and component damage that is permanent; attempting to power on is the most common cause of total loss.
  • ✗Severely mold-affected items — Items with active visible mold growth that have been wet for more than 72 hours typically cannot be restored to a safe, clean condition.
  • ✗Food & perishables — Any food products, opened containers, or items stored at floor level in a sewage or floodwater event must be discarded per California health code.
⚠️

Los Angeles-specific note: California's dry climate means that items left in a flooded home can appear dry on the surface while retaining dangerous levels of moisture inside. Do not attempt to evaluate salvageability yourself before a professional inspection — secondary mold growth can develop within 24–48 hours in the warm LA climate, even in spaces that look dry.

OUR PROCESS

The Atlas Content Restoration Process

A structured six-step process ensures every item is tracked, treated appropriately, and returned in the best possible condition.

1

On-Site Assessment & Triage

Our IICRC certified content specialists walk every affected area, assessing water category, contamination level, and salvageability of each item type. Items are triaged into three categories: restore in-place, pack-out for off-site cleaning, and non-salvageable (documented for insurance).

2

Digital Inventory Documentation

Every item to be packed out is photographed, described, and assigned a unique item number in our digital inventory system. The inventory includes item description, room of origin, pre-restoration condition rating, and estimated replacement cost. You receive a copy of the complete inventory before the truck leaves your property.

3

Professional Pack-Out

Items are carefully wrapped and packed using professional moving materials. Fragile items, artwork, mirrors, and electronics receive individual protection. All boxes are labeled by room and item number, cross-referenced to the digital inventory. Furniture is pad-wrapped and secured for transport to our Los Angeles facility.

4

Content Cleaning & Restoration

At our facility, each item is cleaned using the IICRC-specified method for its material type: dry cleaning for delicate textiles, wet cleaning for washable fabrics, foam cleaning for upholstery, and ultrasonic immersion for hard goods and electronics. Items are sanitized, deodorized, and dried in our controlled environment.

5

Climate-Controlled Storage

Cleaned items are stored in our secure Los Angeles facility until your home is ready. Our storage environment maintains temperature between 60–75°F and relative humidity below 55% — conditions that prevent any secondary moisture damage or mold growth during the structural restoration period. Access to inventory is available by appointment.

6

Return & Final Documentation

Once structural restoration is complete, your belongings are returned, unpacked, and placed in the appropriate rooms per your direction. You sign off on the return inventory, confirming each item was received. Final documentation — including pre- and post-restoration photos, cleaning reports, and the complete inventory — is provided for your insurance file.

CLEANING METHODS

IICRC-Recognized Content Cleaning Techniques

The Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification defines specific cleaning methods for different content types. Atlas Home Pro's IICRC certified technicians apply the right method to every item.

🧴

Dry Cleaning

Uses chemical solvents and low-moisture techniques. Appropriate for delicate fabrics, antique textiles, silk, wool, and items that would be damaged by water-based methods. Common for draperies, formal wear, and heirloom items.

Silk Wool Antique Textiles Formal Wear
💧

Wet Cleaning

Uses water-based cleaning solutions with controlled moisture application and professional drying. The most thorough method for odor elimination and sanitization. Used for cotton, synthetic fabrics, and moderately contaminated items.

Cotton Synthetics Linens Bedding
🫗

Foam Cleaning

Applies a whipped cleaning compound that lifts soil and contamination from the surface without saturating the substrate. Ideal for upholstered sofas, chairs, and area rugs where excessive moisture would cause secondary damage.

Upholstered Sofas Area Rugs Chairs
🌊

Immersion & Ultrasonic Cleaning

Submerges items in a cleaning solution, using ultrasonic agitation for hard surfaces and complex-geometry items. The most thorough cleaning method for electronics circuit boards, HVAC components, hard goods, figurines, and decorative items.

Electronics Hard Goods Decorative Items
📄

Document & Photo Recovery

Wet documents and photographs require immediate attention — drying within the first 24–48 hours prevents permanent damage and mold growth. We air-dry or freeze-dry paper documents, and digitally scan recoverable photographs and important records for permanent backup.

Legal Documents Family Photos Books
💻

Electronics Restoration

We coordinate with certified electronics restoration specialists for televisions, computers, audio equipment, appliances, and smart home devices. The key rule: never power on a wet device. Professional restoration involves disassembly, board cleaning, dielectric treatment, and functional testing before return.

TVs Computers Appliances Audio Equipment

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to what Los Angeles homeowners most often ask about content restoration after water damage.

What items can actually be saved after water damage?

The salvageability of contents depends primarily on two factors: the category of water involved and how quickly restoration begins. Clean water (Category 1) from a supply line break gives the widest window for salvage — upholstered furniture, area rugs, clothing, books, wood furniture, and most hard goods can often be restored if work begins within 24–48 hours. Gray water (Category 2) from dishwashers or washing machines can still yield significant salvage with prompt professional cleaning and sanitization. Black water (Category 3) from sewage or floodwater contaminates porous materials irreversibly — upholstered items, mattresses, and particle-board furniture that have absorbed Category 3 water are typically not salvageable. In Los Angeles, the dry climate is actually an advantage — materials tend to dry faster here than in more humid climates, which extends the restoration window when professionals are called immediately.

Do you store belongings during the restoration process?

Yes. Atlas Home Pro offers full pack-out and secure climate-controlled storage as part of content restoration services. When a home requires structural drying, mold remediation, or reconstruction, belongings often cannot remain on-site. Our team inventories every item before it leaves your property — each piece is photographed, tagged with a unique item number, and logged in a digital inventory system. Items are transported to our secure Los Angeles facility where temperature and humidity are controlled. Clean, restored items are returned when your structure is ready. The inventory log is also provided to your insurance adjuster as part of the claims documentation package, helping support coverage for temporary storage costs, which are typically included under most California homeowners policies as part of additional living expense or personal property coverage.

How do you clean water-damaged furniture and textiles?

Atlas Home Pro uses the cleaning method standards recognized by the IICRC (Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification). The method is matched to the item type and contamination level. Dry cleaning uses chemical solvents for delicate fabrics, silk, wool, and antique textiles that would be damaged by water-based cleaning. Wet cleaning uses water-based solutions with controlled moisture application for cotton, synthetics, and moderately soiled items — this is the most thorough method for odor elimination. Foam cleaning applies a whipped cleaning compound to upholstered items and carpets, lifting soil without saturating the fabric. Immersion cleaning submerges hard goods and textiles in ultrasonic tanks with appropriate solutions — ideal for draperies, heavily soiled fabrics, and hard surfaces. After cleaning, all items are properly dried in a controlled environment before packaging and storage.

Can electronics and appliances be saved after water damage?

Electronics that have been exposed to water can sometimes be restored, but the outcome depends on whether power was applied while wet, the type of water involved, and how quickly they are brought to a professional. The cardinal rule: never attempt to power on a water-damaged electronic device — this causes short circuits and oxidation that permanently destroys components that might otherwise be salvageable. Atlas Home Pro coordinates with certified electronics restoration specialists in the Los Angeles area who can disassemble, clean, and test devices including televisions, home theater equipment, computers, kitchen appliances, and audio equipment. The restoration process involves disassembly, ultrasonic cleaning of circuit boards, dielectric spray treatment, controlled drying, and functional testing. Success rates are highest for devices exposed to clean water that were powered off at the time of damage. For insurance purposes, all electronics are inventoried and photographed before any restoration attempt, so non-salvageable items can be documented for replacement value claims.

How is salvaged content documented for an insurance claim?

Content documentation is one of the most important and most overlooked aspects of a water damage insurance claim. Atlas Home Pro creates a complete contents inventory that includes: a line-item list of every affected item with description, quantity, condition before damage, and estimated replacement cost; pre-restoration photographs of each item showing damage; post-restoration photographs of successfully cleaned and restored items; a written restoration report describing the cleaning methods applied and the outcome; an itemized cost breakdown for all cleaning, pack-out, storage, and restoration services; and a non-salvageable items list for items that could not be restored. This documentation is submitted to your insurance carrier in the format required for contents claims under your homeowners policy. In California, homeowners policies typically cover personal property at actual cash value unless you have a replacement cost rider — knowing the difference matters when your adjuster reviews the contents claim, and our documentation is prepared with that distinction in mind.
