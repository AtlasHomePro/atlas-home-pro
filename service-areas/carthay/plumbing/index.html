SERVING CARTHAY

Why Carthay Homes Need Specialized Plumbing

The roughly 1,000 homes in Carthay — spanning Carthay Circle, South Carthay, and Carthay Square — share a common plumbing heritage: the original galvanized steel and cast iron supply and drain lines installed during construction in the 1920s and 1930s. Unlike copper or PEX, galvanized steel corrodes from the inside, accumulating rust and mineral deposits that narrow flow passages and eventually fail. In Carthay homes that have never been repiped, reduced water pressure throughout the home and rust-tinged water at the taps are reliable warning signs. Sewer lateral replacement is one of the most common major plumbing projects in Carthay. Original vitrified clay sewer pipes on 7,000 sq ft Carthay lots are susceptible to root intrusion from the mature landscaping that gives these streets their distinctive character — the trees on Commodore Sloat Drive, Hayes Drive, and Schumacher Drive are the same age as the pipes beneath them. Trenchless sewer lining and pipe bursting techniques allow us to rehabilitate these laterals in a single day without destroying Carthay's prized front gardens. Water heater replacement is another high-demand service in Carthay. Original bungalow-era utility closets were sized for tank water heaters of a specific era; today's larger tank units often don't fit, making tankless water heaters an ideal solution for Carthay's compact utility rooms while delivering superior efficiency and endless hot water.

How much does sewer line repair cost in Carthay?

Trenchless sewer line lining in Carthay typically costs $3,500 to $7,500 depending on the lateral length and condition. Traditional open-cut repair starts at $5,000 and can exceed $15,000 for long laterals. We recommend a camera inspection first — just $0 service call fee — to determine the best approach.

How often should I inspect plumbing in a Carthay home built in the 1920s?

A plumbing inspection every 3–5 years is advisable for Carthay homes on original galvanized supply lines or clay sewer laterals. Annual sewer camera inspections are recommended if you have large trees near your sewer line — which includes most Carthay properties.

Is the original 1920s plumbing in Carthay homes salvageable?

In some cases, original cast iron drain lines in Carthay are in serviceable condition and can be cleaned and used for many more years. Galvanized supply lines almost always need replacement once a home exceeds 70–80 years old. A pressure test and camera inspection will tell you definitively what you're dealing with.

What water heater do you recommend for a Carthay bungalow?
